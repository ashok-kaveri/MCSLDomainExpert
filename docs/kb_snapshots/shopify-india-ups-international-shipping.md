## What is CSB? Why is it required in International shipping?

**CSB or Courier Shipping Bill** is the main document required by the Customs Authority for allowing any shipment. A Courier shipping bill is issued by the shipping agent and it is considered a certificate for all parties including, the seller, buyer, and third parties (if any). Until 2018, the CSB-II standard was used by merchants. The major drawback with CSB-II was that the merchants were not able to ship high-cost goods. To allow this, the Customs Board (CBEC) issued a notification on August 03, 2018, which states that commercial shipments up to Rs. 5 lacks are allowed using the CSB-V shipping bill.

* * *

## What is the CSB-V shipping label standard?

In simple words, CSB-V is a revision made to the CSB-II standard. With CSB-V, commercial shipments less than Rs. 5 lakhs are allowed through courier mode. The merchants can also avail GST returns once they share the shipping details such as the AWB number and the invoice, which was not possible with the CSB-II standard. The whole standard and the procedures are handled by the Central Board of Indirect Taxes & Customs (CBIC). 

* * *

## How does the CSB-V standard help the merchants?

#### **Less paperwork**

To ship using the CSB-V standard, you just need to have your shipping AWB and invoice. This helps you decrease the paperwork and thereby increase the number of orders that you might be able to complete.

#### **Additional costs only above Rs.25000**

The additional costs for shipping that include Clearance and BRC have to be paid only if the cost of shipments is above Rs.25000.

#### **Customs clearance is much easier**

With the CSB-V standard, you can go through customs clearance, electronically, which is much easier.

#### **Lesser Transit time**

The CSB-V standard allows a much lesser transit time when compared to normal cargo shipping. Shipments via CSB-V take only 2-3 days to reach the customers.

#### **GST Returns**

With CSB-V, you can get GST returns for your international shipments easily. You can get the GST returns once you share the shipping details such as the Airway Bill number (AWB) and the invoice. This is not possible using CSB-II as you do not get a chance to show your shipment as an export.

## Seamlessly handle UPS CSB-V standard shipping in Shopify

With the [**Shopify Multi Carrier Shipping Label app**](https://apps.shopify.com/multi-carrier-shipping-label), you can enable the seamless handle UPS International shipping with CSB-V standard, and print shipping labels & Tax invoices. To enable CSB-V standard with the app, you need to have your own UPS India account. 

Head to the **App settings – > Carriers –> Choose UPS**. Now, configure the UPS India account credentials and select the “Country of origin” as India to see the settings for CSB-V, as shown below.

**1\. TIN Number (or Tax ID/GSTIN)** : You need to have a unique **GST number (or Tax ID)** , that will be displayed on the commercial invoice. Enter the GST identification number.

**2\. AD Code** : The **Authorised Dealer Code** (AD Code) is available with the bank in which the merchant has opened the current account for the purpose of export & import. The AD code is mandatory to generate the shipping bill number which is required for customs clearance.

**Follow the below steps to obtain the AD Code.**  
— Approach the bank in which you have maintained your business current account.  
— Write a letter to the bank branch manager for providing the AD Code in the prescribed format.  
— Obtain the AD Code on the bank letterhead in the **DGFT**(Directorate General of Foreign Trade) prescribed format.  
— Once you obtain the letter, register the AD Code at every port from where you will be exporting the goods.

**3\. E-Commerce Shipments** : You have to select the type of shipment from the drop-down list; if it’s an E-Commerce shipment or a Non-E-Commerce shipment. 

**4\. MEIS Shipments** : The MEIS was introduced as an incentive scheme for the export of goods. The incentives are for the goods exported from India. You can claim the MEIS benefits when shipping products from any of these six categories:

a. Leather Footwear  
b. Customized Fashion Garments  
c. Toys  
d. Books  
e. Handloom Products  
f. Handicraft Products

If it doesn’t fall in any of these, select the Non-MEIS option.

**5\. Shipment Under BOND** : Select from the dropdown list, whether your shipment is under BOND, Letter of Undertaking (LUT), or None. If any, enter the number accordingly.

6\. **IEC Number** : IEC number or the **Import-Export Code** is a 10-digit identification number which is mandatory for export from India or Import to India. This is issued by the DGFT (Director General of Foreign Trade).
